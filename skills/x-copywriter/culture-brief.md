# X (Twitter) culture brief for B2B / GTM founders

For a founder coming from polished LinkedIn into X. Compiled from
2026 algorithm and growth research plus stylistic analysis of
@romanbuildsaas, @MichLieben, @itsalexvacca.

---

## 1. Voice register: X vs LinkedIn

LinkedIn rewards polish, authority, "thrilled to share" cadence.
X rewards the opposite. The platform reads as a group chat among
practitioners. If a tweet looks like it was edited by a comms team,
it dies.

**Lowercase is the default for founder Twitter.** Not a rule,
a signal: "i'm typing this on my phone between meetings, not
running it through a comms approval flow." Capital letters at the
start of every sentence read as press release energy. Use lowercase
for casual takes / shitposts / quick jokes. Use sentence case when
you want gravitas (milestone tweet, hot take, hard data). Mix it.

**What works on X:**

- Casual, specific, mildly self-roasting. "spent 3 weeks building
  what claude did in 15 min from a single link" (MichLieben).
- Hard numbers with no preamble. "Gojiberry AI just hit $2M ARR."
  (romanbuildsaas).
- Operator takes that imply scars. "After 3,200+ outbound campaigns
  the most expensive mistake we see is..." (itsalexvacca).

**What flops:**

- Anything opening with "Excited to announce", "Thrilled to share",
  "Big news!". Instant skip.
- Inspirational closers ("keep building, you got this"). LinkedIn
  import.
- Vague reflection without numbers. Specificity or nothing.

The shitposting layer is how you season the feed: 1 in every 4-5
posts is a one-line dunk on the industry, on yourself, on a
screenshot. Pure jokes are fine. They build the "this person is a
real human" trust that makes people read the serious posts.

## 2. Standalone tweet mechanics

**Length:** 280-char ceiling. 71-100 char sweet spot for one-liners.
120-180 for context tweets. Tweets pushed to the full 280 get
~71% lower engagement than tight ones. If you can cut a word, cut it.

**Hooks for solo tweets are different from LinkedIn hooks.** No
"stat opener with payoff in line 2" because there is no line 2 the
algorithm will reveal. The whole tweet ships as one unit. Every solo
tweet must work as both hook and payoff.

**Punctuation.** Periods are fine. Line breaks fine in moderation
(1-2 max in a solo tweet). No em dashes ever. No emoji bullets.
A single inline emoji is tolerated; emoji as decoration is not.

**Numbered listicle solo tweets work** when each line is one short
clause. 3-5 items max in a solo. More than that, make it a thread.

**Reply ratio.** If you post and never reply to comments under your
own tweets, you signal you don't actually live on X. The algorithm
weights replies ~13.5x a like and a reply-from-author chain ~150x a
like. Reply within the first 30-60 minutes to every commenter on
your own tweets. That window sets the post's trajectory.

**Quote-tweets and dunks** are growth tools when used surgically.
QT yourself when you cross a milestone (one tweet adding context to
the original). QT competitors / bad takes only if you can be funny
and specific. Petty dunks reduce reach in 2026.

## 3. Threads

**When to use a thread:** the idea genuinely needs 4-7 beats. If you
can say it in one tweet, do that. 15-tweet mega-threads from 2023
are dead.

**Mechanics:**

- Hook tweet does 100% of the work. Must stand alone. People decide
  to read or not based purely on tweet 1.
- Optimal length: 5-7 tweets.
- Last tweet is a CTA: "follow @handle for more on outbound systems"
  or "RT the first tweet if useful." Threads without a closing CTA
  leave engagement on the floor.
- "1/" numbering is optional. Many high-performers skip it. If you
  use it, skip the "1/" on the hook and start at "2/".

**Three thread archetypes:**

- **Story thread.** "Failed 7 startups. Lost $40k. Got fired on NYE
  in Bali. Here's what i learned."
- **Tactical thread.** "How we built a 23M-email outbound system in
  12 months." Step by step, screenshots, numbers per step.
- **List thread.** "7 cold-email mistakes i see at $1M+ ARR
  companies." Each tweet = one item with a one-line example.

**Hashtags.** Don't use them. Multiple hashtags get a 40% reach
penalty. Even one is neutral at best. Zero hashtags from
high-performing founder accounts.

**Pin a thread.** The pinned thread is your bio. Pick the tactical
or story thread that best captures what you do.

## 4. LinkedIn imports that get killed on sight

Any of these and you read as a tourist:

- "Excited to announce / Thrilled to share / Big news!"
- "Drop a 👇 in the comments" or "Agree?" closer
- "Here are 7 lessons from..." as a standalone (works as a thread
  hook, not solo)
- Career update / job announcement format
- Long broetry with a line break after every sentence
- Emoji bullets (🚀 ✨ 💡 🎯). Inline emoji only, max one per tweet.
- Hashtags. Any.
- Em dashes. Anywhere.
- Tagging 5+ people in one tweet (engagement bait).
- Bold pull-quote screenshots in slide style.
- Carousel posts. They literally don't exist on X. Faking them with
  screenshots in a thread looks like you wandered in from LinkedIn.

## 5. Build-in-public / GTM founder lane

Five content pillars, weighted roughly:

1. **Lessons learned (30%).** Specific, scarred, numbered. "after
   3,200 campaigns: stop selling to the CMO."
2. **Milestone updates (20%).** Numbers with context. Small numbers
   are fine and relatable; share $1k MRR with the same energy as
   $1M.
3. **Behind-the-scenes (20%).** Screenshots of dashboards, support
   replies, inside-the-product, Loom thumbnails. Visuals as proof,
   not decoration.
4. **Struggles / self-roast (15%).** "spent 3 weeks on a thing AI
   does in 15 min." Funny, specific, no pity. Earns the right to
   post wins.
5. **Hot takes (15%).** Industry observation other operators will
   quote-tweet. "'cold email is dead' is said by people who can't
   write cold email."

**Specific numbers are the asset.** MRR, ARR, churn %, CAC, # demos,
# emails, # replies, conversion rates. Round numbers ($2M ARR) for
milestones. Granular numbers (93,605 emails, 90 demos) for weekly
updates.

**Replies to bigger accounts are your single biggest growth lever.**
70/30 split: ~70% of your time on X is replying to accounts 2-10x
your follower count, ~30% is original posts. Target a list of 10-15
accounts (other GTM founders, AI/outbound voices, your customers)
and reply within 15 minutes of their posts. Add a specific data
point or counter-angle, never "great post" or "this 🔥".

**Quote-tweet your own milestones** to keep them resurfacing. Don't
repost the same screenshot; QT the original tweet with new context
("update: 30 days later this is now $X").

**Small account energy is a feature.** Lower polish, faster wit,
more specific numbers than the big accounts can post. Big accounts
post abstract takes; you post the actual receipts.

## 6. Cadence and engagement loop

- **3-5 original posts/day** while under 1k followers. **5-10/day**
  from 1k-10k. Bursting beats spacing; 2-3 tweets in a 30-min window
  outperforms one per hour.
- **20-50 replies/day to bigger accounts.** This is the actual job.
  Posts are the byproduct.
- **First 30-60 min of a tweet sets its trajectory.** Reply to every
  commenter inside that window. If you can't be on X for the hour
  after posting, don't post then.
- **One thread per week.** Saturdays/Sundays underperform on B2B;
  ship threads Tue-Thu, 9-10am US Eastern catches the most operator
  audience.
- **Pin the strongest thread** as your de facto bio.
- **Reply before you post.** Warm up the algorithm; get 10-20
  replies live before dropping your own tweet.
- **Don't quit at month 2.** Most founders bail at week 8. Accounts
  that compound are still posting at month 6-12. Plan 6-month
  minimum before judging traction.

## 7. Reference accounts (stylistic read)

**@romanbuildsaas (Romàn, Gojiberry AI, YC P26).** Metrics-as-
content lane. Tweets are dense data dumps with no preamble:
"Last 30 days: MRR +36%, churn -30%, cold emails 93,605, demos 90."
Sentence case, period at end of clauses, line breaks between data
points. Hooks are the numbers themselves. Threads are tactical
breakdowns of the funnel that produced those numbers. Good model
for weekly product updates.

**@MichLieben (Michel Lieben, ColdIQ CEO, $7M ARR).** Story + meme +
numbers blend. Lowercase for shitposts ("me after spending 3 weeks
building a campaign that AI just did in 15 minutes from a single
link"), sentence case for milestone threads ("Failed 7 businesses.
Lost $40K. Today $4.5M/year agency"). Self-roast humor + hard
contrarian takes. Good model for personality voice.

**@itsalexvacca (Alex Vacca, ColdIQ co-founder, $7M ARR).**
Operator-with-receipts lane. Opens with volume credentials ("After
3,200+ campaigns at ColdIQ..."), follows with one specific mistake
or pattern, ends with the fix. Cleaner punctuation than Mich, less
meme energy, more "tactical operator" register. Good model for
tactical lessons without comedic layer.

The three together cover the spectrum: data (Romàn), personality
(Mich), tactical authority (Alex). Lift Roman's metrics format
directly for weekly updates. Lift Mich's self-roast structure
for the 1-in-5 humor tweet. Lift Alex's "X campaigns. Y mistake.
Z fix." structure for the lessons-learned post.
