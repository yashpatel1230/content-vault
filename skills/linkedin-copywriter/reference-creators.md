# Reference creators (pattern library, not voice anchor)

Your voice is the anchor (see `corpus.md`). These three creators are
pattern libraries. Steal **structures**, **hook formulas**, and **CTA
mechanics**. Do **not** import their tone, profanity, em-dash habits,
emoji-numbering, or filler phrases. Each creator section ends with a
"do not import" list to prevent voice drift.

All three are public LinkedIn accounts. The patterns below are
extracted from their top-performing posts at the time the corpus was
pulled.

---

## Matt Lakajev (mattlakajev) · LinkedIn coach, Sydney

Top posts pulled: 807r, 331r, 322r, 219r, 207r reactions.

**What he does well:**

- **Identity-list hook.** Five short "I do / I don't" lines that flip
  guru orthodoxy, then a one-line reframe. Post 1 (807r): "I drink
  alcohol / I watch trash reality tv / ... / Becuase I only do 1 thing
  to win / I take massive action without asking for permission."
- **Shock-stat reveal.** "I just ran a 2 wk LinkedIn experiment. 0
  commenting + 3x daily posts. The results?" then 3 stat lines.
- **Roast list with no commentary.** Post 9: "These are the 30 worst
  niches on LinkedIn" then 30 numbered niches. No analysis. Lets the
  reader self-identify.
- **Screw-it / IP-giveaway.** Post 3 (322r): "Screw it. Write 8
  LinkedIn books in 2 yrs. Comment BOOKS and I'll send all 8. FREE."
  Ego-anti, raw, generates 586 comments.
- **24-hour link drop.** "Comment X. I'll share the link in 24 hrs."
  Builds anticipation.
- **Punchy mic-drop close.** Single italic-feeling line. "Self Trust.
  It's the only thing you need to win." or "Yuck."
- **Counter-guru framing.** Names the conventional wisdom ("morning
  routine bros", "personal brand gurus"), positions himself against it.

**Hooks worth stealing:** identity-list, shock-stat reveal, roast-list,
24-hr drop, screw-it/IP-giveaway.

**Do NOT import (would drift voice toward Matt's):**

- Em dashes (`—`). Matt uses them as bullet markers. Project ban holds.
- Profanity (f*ck, sh*t, b*tch, dumbass). Most build-in-public voices
  are casual but not profane.
- "Agree?" closer. AI-bait.
- Deliberate typos as authenticity (Becuase, csn, weid).
- "Yuck 🤢" emoji. Off-brand for most authors.
- The over-cynical "everyone's lying" energy if you build in public
  rather than hate-tweet the platform.

## Michel Lieben (michel-lieben) · Founder, ColdIQ ($7M+ ARR)

Top posts pulled: 743r, 743r, 453r, 453r, 447r reactions.

**What he does well:**

- **"Used to take hours, now takes minutes" hook.** Post 1 (743r):
  "Building cold email campaigns used to take hours. Claude Code
  builds them in minutes:" Concrete tool, concrete time delta.
- **Tool-stack-by-layer.** Body of post 1: `data: Clay, Apollo,
  Openmart, LeadsFactory / enrich: FullEnrich, CompanyEnrich, Prospeo
  / signals: PredictLeads, Trigify`. Categorical breakdown beats
  flat lists for shareability.
- **Long technical breakdown with sub-bullets.** Each numbered step
  has 2-4 hyphen sub-bullets. Posts feel like documentation, not a
  pitch.
- **System-as-brain framing.** "Your folder structure becomes your
  GTM brain. Skills + API calls = reusable infrastructure that
  compounds."
- **Named-operator credits.** Every system-share post names the team
  member who built it ("Ivan Falco, our Head of Growth, built these
  based on 300+ hours running ad campaigns") and ends with a "shoutout
  to X for the cheat sheet." Distributes credit, builds team brand.
- **Double-offer P.S.** Main offer in body ("Comment 'CONTENT' for
  the cheat sheet"). Then a second offer in P.S. ("P.S. We also
  recorded a 40-min video. Lmk if you'd like to watch it"). Two
  hooks per post.
- **Build-in-public revenue anchor.** Posts open with the number that
  earned authority. "We manage > $300k/mo in ad spend." "$151K MRR
  added in 87 days." "70,000 followers, $7M ARR."

**Hooks/structures worth stealing:** "used-to-take-hours" hook,
tool-stack-by-layer body, system-as-brain framing, double-offer P.S.,
named-operator credits.

**Do NOT import:**

- 1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣ keycap emoji as numbered headers. Use `1)` or `1.`,
  plain numerals. Keycap emoji read as AI/template slop.
- Unicode bold (`𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗳𝗼𝘂𝗻𝗱𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻`). LinkedIn renders it but it's
  fake formatting, banned in slop scrub.
- "h.t Monika" shorthand.
- "Lmk" / "Plently" / "Lmk if you'd like to view it too & I'll share
  it" closers. Too many qualifiers.
- The triple repetition of similar posts (Michel reposts the same
  copy multiple times). Don't.

## Fivos Aresti (fivosaresti) · Co-Founder, Workflows.io

Top posts pulled: 290r, 243r, 233r, 181r, 164r reactions.

**What he does well:**

- **Framework declaration hook.** "The 2026 GTM Flywheel.", "The
  evolution of GTM from 2010-2026." Names a thing the post will
  define. Branded-framework framing.
- **Era timeline body.** Year-range header → `↳` tool list → 1-line
  summary. Post 2 (243r): "2010-2014: The Foundation / ↳ Salesforce,
  HubSpot, Marketo / CRMs and marketing automation became the
  standard for B2B." Six eras, each the same shape. Visually
  scannable, infinitely repostable.
- **Market map.** Categorize 50-70 tools/companies into tiers
  (Favorites, Rising, Unicorns, Others). Country flag emoji per
  entry (🇺🇸 🇫🇷 🇬🇧). Closes with "What did I miss?" for
  engagement-bait that actually works (because it surfaces real
  additions in the comments).
- **Diversification story hook.** "We were 100% reliant on X. Y gave
  us a chance to diversify." Honest about dependence, frames the
  switch as agency-building.
- **Forward-slash numbered list.** `1/`, `2/`, `3/`. Slightly more
  Twitter-thread-feel than `1.` or `1)`. Differentiator.
- **`↳` indent character.** For sub-items under a bullet. Visual
  hierarchy without bold or indent.
- **Client case study format.** "We built a Content + Outbound
  flywheel for Freckle.io that generated 1M+ impressions and 640+
  ICP sign-ups in 6 months." Named client + concrete numbers + time
  window in the hook line. Then numbered breakdown of how.
- **"You're still early" close.** "Out of the 1B users on LinkedIn,
  only 1% post weekly. You're still early to get the algo benefits."
  Permission/reassurance close instead of CTA.
- **Build-in-public milestone post.** Post 6: "I just hit 30,000
  followers... 10 best tips on how to grow on LinkedIn:" then
  numbered tactical list. Milestone → tactical handoff.

**Hooks/structures worth stealing:** framework declaration hook, era
timeline body, market-map post type, diversification story, client
case study format, "you're still early" close.

**Do NOT import:**

- 1️⃣ 2️⃣ keycap emoji headers. Same as Michel.
- The 30-stage mega-post length when 5 stages would do. Match length
  to substance.
- "Tons of trial and error" / "honestly" filler at the rate Fivos
  uses them.
- The slightly LinkedIn-coachy posts about "5/ Focus on giving
  value-first / People can smell a shallow AI post". Generic.
